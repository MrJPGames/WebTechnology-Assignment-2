Economics

The economics related to IT security is an important field. You could ask yourself the question - how do companies look at security? Do they even bother hiring experts? How good is their defence mechanisms? Is any of this related to their market share? We will discuss this matter in the following sections.

Enterprises as victims

According to a report from Kaspersky Lab, enterprises in the United States lost around $1.3 million on average in 2017 due to cyber attacks! Globally, the cost of a data breach for enterprises has risen 11 percent in 2017. Overall, businesses are looking at IT security as more of an investment in 2017. In fact, IT security budgets are up, reaching 18 percent for enterprises compared to 16 percent in 2016. Even small businesses with fewer resources are investing more in IT security budgets this year â€” 14 percent compared to 13 percent in 2016. Those are very high numbers and a lot of money goes around with this matter. After businesses increase IT security budgets, 39 percent goes toward protecting increasingly complex IT infrastructure. Improving the level of specialist security experts is another important expenditure, up to 32 percent in 2017 compared to 29 percent in 2016.[1]

Start-ups taking advantage

Back in 2013, some start-up companies tried to take advantage of the security breaches of other companies. A famous start-up was Telegram. It is a messaging service such as Google Allo or WhatsApp is. It's main motivation was the end-to-end encryption mechanism that allows people to chat in 'secret' with one another. It uses a symmetric encryption protocol called MTProto.[2]

Another important emergence of a start-up was MEGA (cloud storage and file hosting service), created by Mega Limited. It is basically a website based in New-Zealand. Its main drive (and thus) marketing strategy was the advertisement that all the files get encrypted, locally before they are uploaded on the cloud. They even grant 50GB of free storage space. This start-up has made a lot of impression on people since it received thousands of user registrations in just the first day of its launch. Ofcourse, because of this, their profits were enormous. There are ofcourse more examples of successful start-ups.[3]
